Our Mission

The purpose of Paw Patrol is to assist homeless animals and provide care and placement services locally across Charlotte. Paw Patrol is a non-profit "all foster home" organization that is willing to provide the necessary resources to all animals in need of rescue. There are many animals out there in need of proper treatment and we would love to get them happy, healthy, and into new homes.

Values

We believe that all animals that come into our care should be saved, which is a philosophy based on no-killing. No animal should be euthanized in our communities if they are able to be care for, behaviorally sound, and healthy. No quality of life shall be taken from an animal unless those animals pose a threat to the public's safety.

Goals

The goal is to make sure that the animals are placed in loving homes based on their needs and that they will not be abandoned or mistreated.

Volunteers

  • Our volunteers come together, and create search groups for animals in poor conditions.
  • A few of the activities they participate in include grooming, playing, feeding, and training.
  • They help with teaching the young about how to care for the animals.

Our History

  • Paw Patrol was founded on August 7, 2017. The organization just turned 5 years!
  • Served in 3 locations:
    - University City Blvd
    - The Plaza
    - Sugar Creek
  • In 2019, we opened our very first animal shelter and adoption center.
  • In 2021, we expanded our facility to include a wellness clinic, spay/neuter clinic, and have grown our adoption center.
